By Directorate of Urban Land Transport



Overview

Bicycling has picked up traction in Bengaluru city during this COVID-19 crisis. As a socially distanced personal transport option it is by far the best option for distances that cannot be covered by walk.

DULT has been at the forefront of mobilising the cycling community over the past 6 years via the #CycleDay initiative to increase the adoption of bicycling as a preferred mode. It has taken up bicycle infrastructure planning as a priority initiative from this year and is committing resources to make this work.

In one of the first of its kind in the country it has designed and working with BBMP to put up 34kms of Pop-Up bicycle lanes on the Outer Ring Road segment between Silk Board jn. and Lowry jn. near KR Puram.

#MapYourCyclingRoute

In order to extend the efficacy of this segment and cover the city, DULT has planned to create Cycling Districts in phases which will extend to the rest of the city over time. The first cycling district spanning approximately 25 plus wards will complement this Pop-Up Bicycle Lane on IRR and enhance the efficacy. The Bengaluru Smart City Ltd. along with BBMP will take up these plans for implementation.

How do we do it?

DULT seeks the help of the public, especially the cycling enthusiasts to define the local use cases across the following categories

Categories Cycle to Work Routes - Purple 145 Cycle to School Routes - Red line 145 Cycle to Transit Routes - Orange line 50 Cycle to Rejuvenate Routes - Green line 8 Cycle to Shop Routes - Blue line (Neighborhood cycling trips) 8

Citizens who use a bicycle for accomplishing any of the above tasks in the demarcated boundary (either living/working in these neighborhoods) are requested to:

Understand

Look at the map to see if your area falls in and around the first identified Cycling District. Observe points of interest in the different layers across work, school, rejuvenate, last mile for transit, local neighborhood shopping activities

Mark

Mark the routes you use on your bicycle for work, school, rejuvenate, last mile for transit, local neighborhood shopping. Mark any points of interest that are not already marked on the map where cyclists might want to have a pit stop.

Upload

Mark them on the map using Google MyMaps, export them as KML or track your ride on a tracking tool like Strava, export the trips as a GPX file and send it to us

How much time do we have?

We will keep this crowdsourcing effort open till 12th August 2020. If your area is not covered in this cycling district it will open up as soon as the plans and budget for the Cycling District 1 is completed and submitted for implementation. Keep a watch out for a notification in this regard on our website and social media channels. Looking forward to covering the entire city and making Bengaluru a cycling friendly city.

Data Collection Methodology Identify routes which you would use to cycle to your workplace, schools, to the nearest bus stops, for a relaxing ride or for health in the mornings, for local grocery shopping in your neighborhood. Mark routes on the map using tools . Find instructions here. The layers that DULT will put out includes ward boundaries, Transit stations, Parks, Lakes, Schools. Tools to be used for marking routes: like maps.google.com, draw.mapunity.com, Strava.com, Mapmyride.com or any tracking tool which can be downloaded and submitted to us as KML or GPX.
